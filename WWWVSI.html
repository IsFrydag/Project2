World Wide Web Definition: The World Wide Web or WWW refers to online content that is formatted in HTML and accessible via the HTTP/HTTPS protocol.

Founded: In 1989. Tim Berners-Lee, an English scientist, created World Wide Web.

Connectivity and Browsing: Browsing the WWW involves using web browsers (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Safari) to access websites and navigate through hyperlinks to view different web pages.

Type: WWW is more software-oriented.

Protocol: The WWW utilizes the Hyper Text Transfer Protocol (HTTP).

Application: The WWW is utilized to access resources all across the world.

Internet Definition: The Internet is a global network of interconnected computer networks that use the TCP/IP protocol to connect devices worldwide.

Founded: The Internet was founded in the late 1960s.

Connectivity and Browsing: The Internet enables various means of connectivity, including direct connections, wireless networks, satellite links, and more.

Type: The Internet is primarily hardware-based.

Protocol: The Internet utilizes the Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol (TCP/IP).

Application: The Internet has a wide range of applications in entertainment, education, research, navigation, advertising, and banking.
